I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Statistics at the University of Manitoba, under the supervision of Dr. Liqun Wang and Dr. Saman Muthukumarana.

My academic path has been shaped by a strong foundation in statistical theory, computational methods, and machine learning, built upon an M.Sc. in Mathematical Statistics from the University of Tehran and a B.Sc. in Statistics from Allameh Tabataba'i University in Iran.

My research focuses on developing robust and interpretable statistical methods for intractable models, particularly in the context of Bayesian inference, Stein discrepancies, and kernel-based model evaluation. I have proposed and implemented innovative techniques such as the Mode-Sensitive Kernel Stein Discrepancy (MS-KSD) and the Gradient-Free Kernel Conditional Stein Discrepancy (GF-KCSD) to address challenges related to robustness, high dimensionality, and measurement error.

With over eight years of combined academic and professional experience, I have led the development and implementation of statistical and machine learning solutions across diverse domains, including neuroscience, genomics, biomedical research, and time-series forecasting.
