I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Statistics at the University of Manitoba, under the supervision of Dr. Liqun Wang and Dr. Saman Muthukumarana.

My academic path has been shaped by a strong foundation in statistical theory and computational methods, built upon an M.Sc. in Mathematical Statistics from the University of Tehran and a B.Sc. in Statistics from Allameh Tabataba'i University in Iran.

My research focuses on developing robust kernel-based methodologies for Bayesian inference and model validation, particularly for intractable likelihoods and high-dimensional conditional distributions. I have proposed novel frameworks such as the Mode-Sensitive Kernel Stein Discrepancy (MS-KSD) and the Gradient-Free Kernel Conditional Stein Discrepancy (GF-KCSD)—methods designed to improve interpretability, robustness, and statistical power in complex models.

With a deep interest in bridging theory and application, I aim to build statistically principled, computationally efficient, and reproducible tools to address modern data challenges in fields such as biomedical sciences, neuroscience, and machine learning.
